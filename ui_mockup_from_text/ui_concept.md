# UI Concept - iCan

## Purpose
Design an accessible, audio-first interface that lets users discover, play, and manage audiobooks easily across devices. The UI prioritizes inclusion for blind users and people with dyslexia, while also supporting busy users who need quick, efficient listening experiences.

## Users
- Blind/Dyslexia Users: navigate fully with screen readers, voice commands, and clear audio feedback
- Authors/Creators: upload and manage audiobook content, and track performance

## Core Screens
- Home/Dashboard: personalized recommendations, continue listening, and quick access to recent titles
- Library/Search: browse categories, search by title/author/topic, and filter by duration/language/format
- Book Details: view synopsis, narrator options (human/AI), accessibility options, and start listening
- Player Screen: playback controls, speed/pitch/voice settings, bookmarks, and voice-note capture
- Quick Listen Screen: summarized audiobook versions with estimated listen time
- Profile & Settings: account, subscription, offline downloads, accessibility, and device preferences
- Creator Portal: upload audiobooks, manage metadata, and view royalty/engagement metrics
- Admin Console: moderation queue, user management, reports, and platform analytics overview

## Actions
- Sign up, log in, and manage subscription plans
- Search, filter, and open audiobook details
- Start, pause, resume, and adjust playback (speed, pitch, voice)
- Enable accessibility settings (screen reader mode, dyslexia font, high contrast)
- Create/edit bookmarks and voice notes while listening
- Download titles for offline listening and sync progress across devices
- Launch Quick Listen summaries for faster consumption
- Creators upload content and monitor analytics; admins moderate and manage users
